I needed a blog setup that makes writing easy while still signaling strong engineering fundamentals. After comparing Ghost, WordPress, and hand-rolled frameworks, I landed on Astro.

## Constraints I optimized for

1. Publish in under 15 minutes.
2. Keep hosting cost close to zero.
3. Own content in Git.
4. Maintain excellent performance by default.

## Why Astro fit

Astro outputs static HTML first, so pages are fast without extra work. Markdown and MDX let me write naturally, then progressively enhance with components when needed.

That combination keeps the writing workflow simple and the architecture clean.

## What happens next

The blog now has a structure I can keep for years: versioned content, reproducible deploys, and clear separation between content and presentation.

A good stack is not the fanciest one. It is the one that stays out of the way so ideas ship consistently.
