Consistency beats inspiration. I do not wait for perfect ideas. I use a short repeatable process.

## The workflow

1. Capture rough notes from active work.
2. Pick one question a post should answer.
3. Write a first draft in Markdown without editing.
4. Tighten structure and remove filler.
5. Add one concrete example and publish.

## Post template I reuse

I usually follow this shape:

```md
# Title

## Context

## Decision

## Trade-offs

## What changed in practice
```

## Why this works

- It keeps scope tight.
- It avoids over-explaining.
- It creates an archive of decisions I can revisit later.

## A small quality bar

Before publishing, I check three things:

1. Is the key idea clear in the first minute?
2. Is there at least one useful example?
3. Can someone apply this today?

This process keeps momentum high and quality stable.
